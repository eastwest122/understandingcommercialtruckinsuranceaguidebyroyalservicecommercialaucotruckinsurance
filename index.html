What Is Commercial Auto Insurance?

    Commercial auto insurance is a policy designed to protect vehicles used for business purposes. Unlike a personal auto insurance policy—which covers standard cars for personal use—commercial auto insurance covers vehicles such as:

  • Semi trucks and tractor trailers

  • Delivery trucks

  • Commercial vans

  • Trucks hauling goods or equipment

  • Vehicles used to carry people for business

    • These vehicles are exposed to higher liability, greater mileage, more wear and tear, and increased risk due to their business use. That’s why commercial auto insurance has different coverages and requirements than personal auto insurance.

Commercial Truck Insurance vs Commercial Auto Insurance

    These terms are often used interchangeably, but there are distinctions:

  • Commercial Auto Insurance is broader, covering all commercial use vehicles, including vans, pickups, box trucks, etc.

    • Coverages like motor carrier loads, bobtail liability, trailer interchange, and specialized endorsement are more common in commercial truck insurance.

    At Royal Service Commercial Auto & Truck Insurance, we cover both—whether you need commercial auto insurance, or specific commercial truck coverage.

    Common Misconceptions

  • “My personal auto policy covers me when I use the vehicle for business.” Not necessarily. Many personal policies specifically exclude or limit business use coverage.

  • “Cheapest premium equals best insurance.” If coverage is minimal or has many exclusions, it’s not “best.”

  • “One policy fits all my vehicles.” Different vehicles and uses demand different coverages. A semi truck vs a delivery van vs a contractor’s work truck—they each have unique needs.

  • “Claims will always be covered.” Only if you’ve met the policy obligations (reporting accidents, driver qualifications, maintenance). Failing these can lead to denied claims.
